8 Major Food Allergens

Egg

Eggs are one of the most common allergy-causing foods for children.

Symptoms

  • Hive
  • Swelling of the face, lips or eyes
  • Tingling in the mouth
  • Pains in the abdomen or vomiting

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Swelling of the tongue or throat
  • Hoarse voice or difficulty speaking
  • Wheeze or constant cough
  • Becoming pale, floppy or dizzy or collapsing

Dairy Products

Dairy allergy symptoms, which differ from person to person, occur a few minutes to a few hours after you or your child drinks dairy or eats dairy products.

Symptoms

  • Hives
  • Wheezing
  • Itching or tingling feeling around the lips or mouth
  • Swelling of the lips, tongue or throat
  • Coughing or shortness of breath
  • Vomiting

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Constriction of airways, including a swollen throat that makes it difficult to breathe
  • Facial flushing
  • Shock, with a marked drop in blood pressure

Peanut

It is one of the most common causes of a severe allergic attack Even a very small number of peanuts can cause severe reactions to some peanut-allergic people, which can even be life-threatening.

Symptoms

  • Skin reactions, such as hives, redness or swelling
  • Itching or tingling in or around the mouth and throat
  • Tightening of the throat
  • Shortness of breath or wheezing
  • Runny nose

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Constriction of airways
  • Swelling of the throat that makes it difficult to breathe
  • A severe drop in blood pressure (shock)
  • Rapid pulse
  • Dizziness, lightheartedness or loss of consciousness

Tree Nuts

A tree nut allergy is one of the most common food allergy in both adults and children

Symptoms

  • Abdominal pain, including cramping and stomach upset
  • Nausea and/or vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Trouble swallowing
  • Itching of the mouth, throat, skin, eyes, hands, or other body regions
  • Shortness of breath and difficulty breathing
  • Wheezing
  • Nasal congestion or runny nose

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Swollen throat
  • Passing out
  • Trouble swallowing
  • Vomiting
  • A red rash with hives or welts

Sesame

ESesame is a popular ingredient. People can use it in oils, salads, baking, and sushi. But, for some people, sesame seeds and oil cause an allergic reaction.

Symptoms

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Hives
  • Pain in the abdomen
  • Coughing
  • Swelling

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Trouble breathing
  • Fainting
  • Wheezing
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Cardiac arrest
  • Dizziness

Soy & Soy products

Dairy allergy symptoms, which differ from person to person, occur a few minutes to a few hours after you or your child drinks dairy or eats dairy products.

Symptoms

  • Tingling in the mouth
  • Hives; itching; or itchy, scaly skin (eczema)
  • Swelling of the lips, face, tongue and throat, or other body parts
  • Wheezing, a runny nose or breathing difficulty
  • Abdominal pain, diarrhea, nausea or vomiting
  • Skin redness (flushing)

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Difficulty breathing, caused by throat swelling
  • Shock, with a severe drop in blood pressure
  • Rapid pulse
  • Dizziness, lightheartedness or loss of consciousness

Wheat

Wheat allergy is an allergic reaction to foods containing wheat.

Symptoms

  • Swelling, itching or irritation of the mouth or throat
  • Hives, itchy rash or swelling of the skin
  • Nasal congestion
  • Headache
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Cramps, nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Chest pain or tightness
  • Severe difficulty breathing
  • Trouble swallowing
  • Pale, blue skin color
  • Dizziness or fainting

Fish & Shellfish

Allergies to shellfish and fish are more common in adults and adolescents than in young children.

Symptoms

  • Raised red bumps of skin – hives
  • Swelling of the lips
  • Tingling of the throat and mouth
  • Itchy skin and rash
  • Runny nose
  • Tightening of the throat
  • Digestive symptoms – cramps, stomach pain, nausea or vomiting

Severe allergic reaction – anaphylaxis

  • Difficulty or noisy breathing
  • Swelling of the tongue
  • Difficulty talking or a hoarse voice
  • Wheeze or persistent cough
  • Persistent dizziness or collapse
  • Paleness and floppiness in yong children
